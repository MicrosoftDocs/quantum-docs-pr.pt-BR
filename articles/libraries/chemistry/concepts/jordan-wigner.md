<span data-ttu-id="866cc-109">A representação Jordânia-Wigner é direta para derivar.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="866cc-109">The Jordan-Wigner representation is straight forward to derive.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="866cc-113">É fácil verificar se \begin{align} um ^ \dagger_j & = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 \\\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{X_j + iY_j}{2}, \nonumber\\\\ a_j & = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 \\\ 1 & 0 \end{ bmatrix} = \frac{X_j-iY_j}{2}, \end{Align}, em que $X _J $ e $Y _J $ são os operadores Pauli $ $X $ e-$Y $ que atuam em qubit $j $.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="866cc-113">It is easy to verify that \begin{align} a^\dagger_j &= \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 \\\ 0 &0 \end{bmatrix}=\frac{X_j + iY_j}{2}, \nonumber\\\\ a_j &= \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 \\\ 1 &0 \end{bmatrix}=\frac{X_j - iY_j}{2}, \end{align} where $X_j$ and $Y_j$ are the Pauli-$X$ and -$Y$ operators acting on qubit $j$.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="866cc-114">Isso mostra que, para um único orbital de rotação, é fácil representar os operadores de criação e Annihilation em termos de matrizes de unitários que os computadores Quantum entendem.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="866cc-114">This shows that for a single spin orbital it is easy to represent creation and annihilation operators in terms of unitary matrices that quantum computers understand.</span></span>
